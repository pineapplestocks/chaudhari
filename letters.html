First Half 2026: Paid to Wait, Disciplined to Own

June 2026

Beginning value$507,029
June balance$573,428
YTD change+13.1%
S&P 500 YTD+7.4%

Dear Partner,

Through the first half of 2026, the account has continued to progress in the way we hoped it would: not through prediction, leverage for its own sake, or constant activity, but through a disciplined process of underwriting businesses, collecting premium when compensation appeared attractive, and remaining willing to hold cash when the opportunity set was less clear.

Based on the June 27, 2026 Schwab balance letter, account value increased from $507,029 at the start of the year to $573,428, with no deposits or withdrawals. That represents an unaudited year-to-date increase of approximately 13.1%. For context, the S&P 500 was reported up approximately 7.4% year-to-date through June 26, 2026.

What Drove Results

Performance so far has come from a combination of option premium, dividends and interest, and market appreciation in selected holdings. Through May 31, the statements reflected $9,701 of year-to-date dividends and interest, $49,222 of market appreciation, and $2,332 of expenses. The June balance letter suggests continued progress after month-end, with account value increasing by roughly $9,808 from the May statement value.

The strategy remains grounded in a simple standard: we should not sell options on a company we would be unwilling to own at the right price. That standard does not eliminate volatility, but it helps keep activity connected to business ownership rather than speculation.

How We Are Thinking About Risk

Options premium is often described too casually. We do not view it as free income. It is compensation for accepting obligation, timing risk, and the possibility of owning or parting with securities at prices set in advance. During the first half, covered calls and cash-secured puts helped create defined entry and exit points, but they also required discipline around position sizing, collateral, and liquidity.

We continue to prefer a structure that is understandable and resilient. Cash and cash-equivalent collateral matter. So does the willingness to say no when the premium available does not justify the risk being accepted.

Looking Forward

We do not expect returns to arrive smoothly, and we will not manage the partnership around short-term comparisons. The goal is to compound capital carefully over time by combining business judgment, valuation discipline, and emotional control.

Our job is to remain selective. When markets offer attractive compensation for risks we understand, we may act. When they do not, patience is not a lack of activity; it is part of the process.

Thank you for your trust and interest in Chaudhari Partners.
