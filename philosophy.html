Investment philosophy

Ownership before activity.

We believe durable results come from underwriting businesses carefully, keeping obligations collateralized, and waiting for risk and reward to become attractive enough to act.

Our standard

We are paid to wait only when the wait is underwritten.

Chaudhari Partners Family Fund is built around a simple idea: options should begin with ownership judgment, not speculation. Before we accept an obligation, we ask whether the underlying business or security is one we understand, whether the price provides a margin of safety, and whether the collateral required is worth committing.

Cash is not treated as a drag. It is inventory for future decisions, protection against forced selling, and the foundation that allows the strategy to remain patient. We would rather look inactive than accept risks that cannot be explained plainly.

Business Quality First

We start with the underlying business. If we would not be comfortable owning it at a rational price, we have no interest in selling puts simply because the premium looks attractive. The option is a tool; the business remains the asset being underwritten.

Collateral Before Leverage

The strategy is designed around cash-secured obligations and defined exposure. Collateral gives us room to think clearly, accept assignment when planned, and avoid being pushed into decisions by market volatility or margin pressure.

Valuation Discipline

A high-quality company can still be a poor investment at the wrong price. We look for situations where the strike price, premium received, and business value create an entry point we would be willing to defend even if the market moves against us.

Premium Is Compensation, Not Free Income

Options premium is earned by accepting obligation, timing risk, liquidity risk, and the possibility of ownership. We want to be compensated for risks we can define in advance, rather than tempted by yield for its own sake.

Patience Is Part of the Process

There will be periods when the right decision is to do less. Holding cash, passing on marginal ideas, or allowing opportunities to expire can be just as important as entering a position. Activity is not the goal; attractive risk-adjusted ownership is.

Risk Management

Risk is not only volatility. It includes permanent capital loss, poor business quality, excessive leverage, crowded positioning, emotional decision-making, and being forced to sell at the wrong time. We try to manage risk before entering the position, not after the market has already tested it.

Long-Term Alignment

We are building for partners who think in years, understand that returns will not arrive evenly, and value clear reasoning over constant market commentary. The right partner prefers discipline over activity and ownership over speculation.
